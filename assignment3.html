Snack Clips

We have lots of snacks at home and we always struggle with not have enough clips to keep them fresh, so I made different variety of the clips.

Big Four Leaf Clover Clip

This one used the technique of printing the leaf and the clip separately. The back of the leave is indented with a rectangle polysurface. The front of the clip is portruding with the same size rectangle polysurface. I tried different ways to make this assemble piece.

  • Have the indentation on the clip instead of the flower as I thought that might make the quality comes out better without having the portruding piece.
  • Tried different rotation of the clip to sit on the bed to reach optimal results.
  • Adjusted the tolerance of the clip and the leaf to make them better assemble together.

Indented Four Leaf Clover Clip

I reversed this version by indented the four leaf clover pattern on the clip instead.

  • Indented the leaf on the clip instead to see if the result is better.
  • This also safes time as the big leaf version was pretty but very time consuming.

Nested Spheres

3 Layer Nested Spheres

This took me forever, I tried many different ways to do things in grasshopper as much as possible, but failed again and again to reach the ideal result. The final result here is not super idea either, but it's definitely getting closed, and my conclusion is some of the work is just bettern done in Rhino. I end up deleted lots of my grasshopper code and new grasshopper functions I was trying out since I was not able to make it work.

I used grasshopper for all the offset work, then baked the spheres I needed and cut them in Rhino.
