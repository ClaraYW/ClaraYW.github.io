2CM Cubes

Model / Slice / Print

I modeled the a 2 square cm cube in Rhino, then sliced them in Cura4.8 with different settings. After that, printed with Ender 3 pro and measured each cube with clipers. I tried different build plate adhesion, skirt, brim and none. Brim did the best job to help making the bottom of the object looks nice as it helped to make sure the melted plastic sticks well each layer as the nozzel goes up. I also adjusted the initial layer print speed to 10mm/s to help with adhesion issues.

3CM Tubes

Model / Slice / Print

In rhino, I modeled two 3 square cm tubes, one with a wall of 0.8mm thick, the other with a wall of 1.6mm thick. I then sliced them in Cura4.8 with different settings and printed with Ender 3 pro and measured each cube with clipers. I originally thought I could adjust the wall thickness in Cura by changing the wall line count. After printed out with the different wall line count setting, I noticed there weren't much difference between the size, it certainly wasn't double. Junchao helped to explained to me that the wall line count in Cura is auto calcualted based on the desired total thickness of the object, it's not going to double the thickness of the tube. I then made another model in Rhino, offset the surface with twice of the value, and printed the version two.

3CM Cylinders

Model / Slice / Print

Below are the key takeaways for these sets of work:

  • Increasing the tolerance level can create plat surface to a supposed to be rounded surface.
  • You can define the Z-steam alignment either use the shortest line or random or define a specific posiiton.
  • Infill Density impacts the speed of the print, lower density will print faster.
  • Spiralize Outer Countour was cool effect. You can easily make a cup with this.
  • Played with a few different support type, good to see what are the available settings in Cura for support.

2CM Cube with A Hole

Model / Slice / Print

I tried 3 times for this task. I didn't finish printing the first 1 as I noticed the line was way too thin which cause it to not stick very well.

Attempt 1 Settings

  • Standard Quality
  • Line Width 0.2mm
  • Wall lined width:0.2mm
  • Initial Layer Speed - 20mm/s
  • Brim - 10 line counts
  • Z Seam Alignment - Random
  • Infill Pattern - Cross

Attempt 2 Settings

  • Increase line Width to 0.4mm
  • Decrease initial Layer Speed to 10mm/s
  • Increase Brim to 15 line counts

Attempt 3 Settings

  • Decrease innerfill line Width to 0.3mm
  • Change infill pattern to grid to make it more solid
  • Change top/bottom pattern to zig zag
